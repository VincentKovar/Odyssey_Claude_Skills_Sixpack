# Performance Creative Strategy Skill

This skill governs all creative strategy work. Read and apply these principles before writing any brief, script, or concept. When in doubt: **research first, write second, show proof always.**

---

## Core Belief

Every good insight can be deployed across any format. The goal is not one great ad â€” it's a **diversified creative system** that is always expanding and always exploring.

**Small iterations are dead.** Use Expand vs. Explore:
- **Expand:** Scale what's working â€” new formats, audiences, or angles for a winning concept
- **Explore:** Try something genuinely new, grounded in research

## The #1 Creative Mandate: Undeniable Proof

**Every ad, whenever possible, must contain a moment of undeniable proof.**

The viewer is skeptical. They've tried other products. They don't believe you yet. Your job is to show â€” not tell â€” proof that the product delivers on its promise. Make it so visually obvious that the viewer cannot argue with it.

### What Undeniable Proof Looks Like

Undeniable proof is a **visual demonstration** that makes the product's benefit self-evident. The best reference is always *the product working*.

| Product Type | Undeniable Proof Example |
|---|---|
| Filtered shower head | Crack open the old filter and show the filth inside â€” that dark, disgusting buildup IS the proof your water was contaminated |
| Car insurance app | Real person, real phone, running through the app live, watching the low rate appear on screen |
| Lawn care | Real before/after of an actual lawn â€” dead and patchy â†’ thick and lush â€” shot in a believable, non-stock way |
| Non-stick cookware | Egg slides off with no oil, clean as glass |
| Supplement | Real before/after photos with visible transformation, timestamped or dated |
| Skincare | Side-by-side skin texture close-up before and after use |

### Proof Formats That Work

- **Before/After** â€” real transformation, shown honestly (not stock)
- **Live product demo** â€” show it working in real time, no cuts during the key moment
- **Physical reveal** â€” open, cut, pour, or expose something hidden (dirt, buildup, the inside of a filter)
- **Screen recording** â€” real app, real results, real numbers on screen
- **Side-by-side comparison** â€” competitor vs. product, or before vs. after
- **Reaction shot** â€” genuine surprise or relief at the result (not acted)
- **Measurement/data** â€” lab results, numbers, statistics shown on screen

### Proof Commandment

> If a claim is being made in the ad, ask: "What would make this claim *visually undeniable*?" Then show that.

Never allow a finished brief or script to make a claim without a corresponding proof moment. If no proof moment exists, flag it and find one before briefing.

## The Andromeda Problem

Ad accounts naturally drift toward one creative style. Fight this with deliberate diversity across:
- **Formats** (native, elevated, traditional, static/dart)
- **Angles** (problem, outcome, social proof, founder, etc.)
- **Audiences** (cold, warm, retargeting, demographics)

**Rule:** If that format + angle + audience combo has been done â€” do something new. Never run three versions of the same idea.

## The 10 Creative Commandments

**1. Know exactly who this is for**
Define the person: demographics, problem, buying stage. Buying stage determines the entire message. Never write for a vague "target audience."

**2. Find the Single Most Important Thing (SMIT)**
One message. One promise. One thing the viewer walks away knowing. If a brief has two SMITs, it has none.

**3. Use a storytelling framework**
Every piece of creative follows a recognizable structure: AIDA, PAS, QUEST, Before/After, Desired Outcome, etc. Frameworks create narrative gravity.

**4. Leverage consumer psychology**
Connect to at least one of the 7 core human desires:
1. Financial success
2. Social approval
3. Longer lifespan
4. Sexual companionship
5. Higher status in society
6. Protection of loved ones
7. Freedom from fear or pain

**5. Write in the audience's language**
Use customer research to find exact words real customers use. Be specific â€” "PCOS" not "fertility issues." Specificity is the shortcut to trust.

**6. Use references to accelerate creation**
Every piece of creative needs 1 hero reference. 60â€“80% similarity to the reference is fine. Use Foreplay to find and share references.

**7. Respect the audience's attention**
"If there is ever a hint of boredom, I cut it." â€” Mr. Beast. Something new every 1â€“2 seconds: cut, camera effect, transition, animation.

**8. Start with the most compelling thing (Inverted Storytelling)**
Show the end result right at the beginning. You have 1 second. Stop thinking chronologically. Lead with the single most compelling idea, then tell the story.

**9. Show undeniable proof**
The viewer is skeptical. Show proof: real customer results, before/after, lab data, visible physical transformation, real-time product performance. The best reference is the product working. Show it working. See [Undeniable Proof section](#the-1-creative-mandate-undeniable-proof) above.

**10. Show macro shots so the viewer can "shop" the product**
They can't touch it. Use close-up shots to recreate the sensory experience digitally â€” texture, quality, freshness, precision.

## Brief Types â€” When to Use What

**Brief mix target:** ~30% Word-for-Word / ~60% Hybrid / ~10% Open Ended

| Brief Type | What It Is | Use When | Not For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Word-for-Word Script** | Full script: concept summary, hero reference, VO, captions, visual action, extra hooks | Elevated shoots; traditional UGC creators; actors | Top 1% creators (over-constrains them) |

| **Open Ended Brief** | No script. Product summary, filming instructions, hook thought-starters, good/bad references | Big swing creative; top-tier creators; monthly affiliate brief | Actors or traditional UGC creators without proven ability |

**Format â†’ Brief type matching:**
- Street Interview â†’ Hybrid Brief

- Open Ended Creator Brief â†’ Open Ended (1 per brand per month)

## Aesthetic Direction

What great performance content looks like:
- Native iPhone-quality video â€” not studio-polished
- Real people in real environments (cars, bedrooms, kitchens, outdoors)
- Short, punchy text overlays integrated into the edit
- Authentic emotional reactions â€” surprise, relief, excitement
- Relatable everyday scenarios where the product fits naturally
- **Specific proof moments** â€” the product doing something visibly, undeniably real

What to avoid:
- Ads that look like ads (professional lighting, VO announcers, branded lower thirds)
- Empty vague claims ("this changed my life") without proof
- Studio-shot product lifestyle content without a human story
- Anything that would feel out of place in an organic TikTok feed
